## Design Context

### Users

Kids and teens (roughly 8–16) trading Match Attax football cards with friends — casual, social,
schoolyard-trading energy translated into a mobile PWA. They browse the card catalog, manage a
"Haves"/"Wants" inventory, list cards for trade, negotiate over real-time chat, and check a
fairness score before agreeing. Mostly used one-handed on a phone, in short bursts (between
classes, on the sofa, in a group chat with friends) — not at a desk.

### Brand Personality

Kinetic and screen-native: a dark, neon-accented "trading dashboard" energy — the app itself feels
like a piece of collector tech, not a printed card brought to the screen. Levels, streaks, and
achievement badges are an intentional, embraced part of the experience (not something to avoid),
because progression and collecting go hand in hand for this audience.

### Aesthetic Direction

Dark-only by design — there is no light theme. Near-black background family (`#101418` and
neighboring surface tones), with four vivid accent hues doing all the signaling: cyan (interactive/
primary), yellow (warning/a rarity tier), pink (brand mark/a rarity tier), and orange (a rarity
tier). Cards are fully rounded (no clipped-corner motif) with a diagonal gradient fill and a
rarity-colored border + glow — rarity is a first-class visual property of every card surface, not
just a small text chip. Headings in Space Grotesk, body in Inter.

### Design Principles

1. **Rarity reads at a glance** — every card-shaped surface (catalog tile, inventory tile, hero)
   carries a border + glow colored by rarity tier, not just a chip; the 7 real `cards.rarity` values
   map onto the 4 vivid accents plus two muted tones (see `lib/cards/rarity.ts`).
2. **Dark-only, single source of truth** — no light/dark branching anywhere; every token lives in
   one `:root` block in `app/globals.css`. Don't reintroduce theme-switching without deliberately
   revisiting this.
3. **Progression is real, not decorative** — level/XP, streaks, and achievement badges are backed
   by real data (`lib/gamification/`, `activity_log`/`achievements`/`user_achievements` tables), not
   placeholder mock values.
4. **Desktop gets a sidebar, mobile gets a top bar + bottom tabs** — `components/nav/Sidebar.tsx`
   (desktop) vs. `MobileTopBar.tsx` + `MobileNav.tsx` (mobile); don't reintroduce a top header nav.
5. **Trust signals for trading** — the fairness meter, chat, and accept/decline actions still need
   enough visual seriousness that a trade reads as fair and safe, even inside a playful shell.
