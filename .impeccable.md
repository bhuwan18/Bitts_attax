## Design Context

### Users

Kids and teens (roughly 8–16) trading Match Attax football cards with friends — casual, social,
schoolyard-trading energy translated into a mobile PWA. They browse the card catalog, manage a
"Haves"/"Wants" inventory, list cards for trade, negotiate over real-time chat, and check a
fairness score before agreeing. Mostly used one-handed on a phone, in short bursts (between
classes, on the sofa, in a group chat with friends) — not at a desk.

### Brand Personality

Card-pack-opening drama: punchy, glossy, boastful — the energy of pulling a good card, not reading
a stats dashboard. Levels, streaks, and achievement badges are an intentional, embraced, *loud*
part of the experience — progression should feel like a small win each time, with motion and scale
to match, not a quiet counter ticking up.

**First pass shipped too sparse/flat** relative to this intent — a dark palette alone isn't
"kinetic." The fix is bolder type contrast, real motion (not just a hover transition), and more
things happening on screen at once, not just bigger versions of the same few widgets.

### Aesthetic Direction

Dark-only by design — there is no light theme. Near-black background family (`#101418` and
neighboring surface tones), with four vivid accent hues doing all the signaling: cyan (interactive/
primary), yellow (warning/a rarity tier), pink (brand mark/a rarity tier), and orange (a rarity
tier). Cards are fully rounded with a diagonal gradient fill and a rarity-colored border + glow —
rarity is a first-class visual property of every card surface, not just a small text chip, and for
the top two tiers (`legend`/`limited`) the glow ambiently pulses rather than sitting static.

**Typography**: display headlines and big numbers (OVR ratings, stats, level) in **Bungee** —
chunky, blocky, stadium-scoreboard/foil-sticker energy, used sparingly for hero moments, not body
copy. Body/UI text in **Manrope** — clean and warm, enough contrast against Bungee's geometric
weight to keep long text readable. (Space Grotesk + Inter, the first pass's pairing, are both
flagged as overused "AI-default" fonts — don't reach back for either.)

**Motion is load-bearing, not decoration**: staggered entrance choreography on every list/grid
(`animate-in fade-in slide-in-from-bottom-4`, per-item delay via inline `animationDelay`), hover
states that lift + scale + intensify glow, and a genuine celebration moment (`CelebrationBurst`,
`components/gamification/`) for streak/achievement/rare-pull moments — not just a toast. Avoid
bounce/elastic easing (reads as dated); lean on `ease-out-quint`/`expo` for a confident, weighted
deceleration instead.

### Design Principles

1. **Rarity reads at a glance, and the top tiers announce themselves** — every card-shaped surface
   (catalog tile, inventory tile, hero) carries a border + glow colored by rarity tier; `legend`/
   `limited` additionally pulse ambiently. The 7 real `cards.rarity` values map onto 4 vivid accents
   plus two muted tones (see `lib/cards/rarity.ts`).
2. **Dark-only, single source of truth** — no light/dark branching anywhere; every token lives in
   one `:root` block in `app/globals.css`. Don't reintroduce theme-switching without deliberately
   revisiting this.
3. **Progression is real AND celebrated** — level/XP, streaks, and achievement badges are backed by
   real data (`lib/gamification/`, `activity_log`/`achievements`/`user_achievements` tables) *and*
   get an actual moment (motion, scale, sound-adjacent visual punctuation) when they change, not a
   silent number update.
4. **Motion has a job, and a budget** — every animation should convey entrance, state change, or
   feedback; nothing loops forever except the two top-rarity glow pulses and the streak flame.
   `prefers-reduced-motion` always gets a crossfade-only fallback, never "no motion at all" for
   functional feedback (unlock toasts still need to appear).
5. **Desktop gets a sidebar, mobile gets a top bar + bottom tabs** — `components/nav/Sidebar.tsx`
   (desktop) vs. `MobileTopBar.tsx` + `MobileNav.tsx` (mobile); don't reintroduce a top header nav.
6. **Trust signals for trading** — the fairness meter, chat, and accept/decline actions still need
   enough visual seriousness that a trade reads as fair and safe, even inside a playful shell.
