## Design Context

### Users

Kids and teens (roughly 8–16) trading Match Attax football cards with friends — casual, social,
schoolyard-trading energy translated into a mobile PWA. They browse the card catalog, manage a
"Haves"/"Wants" inventory, list cards for trade, negotiate over real-time chat, and check a
fairness score before agreeing. Mostly used one-handed on a phone, in short bursts (between
classes, on the sofa, in a group chat with friends) — not at a desk.

### Brand Personality

Energetic & sporty. Three words: **kinetic, competitive, pack-fresh** — the rush of ripping open a
new pack, the confidence of a matchday broadcast graphic, not a toy. Explicitly NOT cheap/gamified
(no confetti, cartoon mascots, badge-collecting UI) and NOT generic SaaS (no Inter-on-gray dashboard
look). Reference feel: sports broadcast lower-thirds, stat overlays, team badge design — bold
numerals, dynamic angles, confident color used sparingly — rather than a kids'-game shell.

### Aesthetic Direction

Theme-aware: both light and dark are first-class (this app already ships `next-themes`), so every
color decision must be authored as OKLCH tokens that work in both directions, not a single fixed
palette. Visual language draws from football-broadcast graphics and physical trading cards (foil,
stat blocks, OVR ratings) rather than mobile-game UI. Anti-references: gradient-fill text, neon
cyan/purple "AI" palettes, confetti/cartoon gamification, generic admin-dashboard chrome.

### Design Principles

1. **Sporty confidence, not toy playfulness** — bold type and a punchy accent color, used with
   restraint (60/30/10 weighting), not saturation everywhere.
2. **Card-first visual hierarchy** — the trading cards themselves (rarity, OVR, team) are the hero;
   surrounding UI chrome (nav, forms, chat shell) stays quiet so cards pop.
3. **Theme-aware from the start** — light and dark are equally real; tint neutrals toward the brand
   hue in both, don't just invert a light palette.
4. **Mobile-first touch ergonomics** — this lives in kids' hands on phones; thumb-reach bottom nav,
   generous tap targets, and one-handed flows matter more than desktop chrome.
5. **Trust signals for trading** — the fairness meter, chat, and accept/decline actions need enough
   visual seriousness that a trade still reads as fair and safe, even inside a playful shell.
